identity

World Renowned Architect

Luis Barrágan is a world renowned architect, having achieved numerous accalaides within the design community. Barrágan is known for his simplistic design, bold color, and emphasis on utilizing nature to create a central outdoor space. Some of his achievements include the Pritzer Architectural Prize, and having work featured in UNESCO’s world heritage site.

  • 1902, March 9th

    Luis Barragán date of Birth

    Luis Ramiro Barragán Morfín was born to a wealthy family in Jalisco, Mexico. Barragan grew up between the Mexican city Guadalajara and his family ranch.

  • 1923

    Barragán Graduation from Escuela Libre

    Barragán graduates with an education in civil engineering and architecture from from Escuela Libre de Ingenieros.

  • 1945

    Barragán begins Jardines del Pedgregal

    Between 1945 and 1952, Barragán designed the complex Jardines del Pedgregal for the firm Jardines del Pedgregal de San Angel, S.A.. His focus was to preserve the natural beauty of the surrounding landscape.

  • 1948

    Barragán builds Casa Luis Barragán

    Located near the town of Tacubaya, a working-class area, Barragán demonstates his desire for functionality and livabily as shown in his home, Casa Luis Barragán.

  • 1957

    Barragán invited to design Ciudad Satélite

    Cuidad Satélite was to be built as the symbol of urbanization, which Barragán conceptualizaed using vertical elements, and invited sculptor Mathías Goeritz to collaborate.

  • 1964

    Barragán builds Cuadra San Cristóbal

    In collaboration with Andrés Casillas, Barragán builds Cuadra San Cristobal, an equestrian estate in Mexico City. This project would go on to become one of his most recognized works.

  • 1976, June 4th

    Launch of Museum of Modern Art Exhibit

    "MoMA" displayed the first exhibit of Barragán's work through a catalogue published by Emilio Ambasz. This exhibit advanced Barragán to international fame and recoginition.

  • 1980

    Barragán awarded The Pritzker Prize

    The Pritzker Architectural Award, considered to be the highest achievement in architecture, honored Barragán for his "submline act of the poetic imagination" and his creation of "gardens, plazas, and fountains of haunting beauty—metaphysical landscapes for meditation and companionship."

  • 1988, November 22

    Luis Barragán Death

    Barragán passed away at the age of eighty-six in his Tacubaya residence, in Mexico City.
