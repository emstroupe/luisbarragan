background

Cuadra San Cristóbal was built by in 1968 for the Egerston family in Mexico City. The home was the designed as a modernist ranch, which Barrágan consulted on with the family. The estate functions as a home, but also a fully equipped stables. As seen buy photographers and artists alike, horses and ranch life as highlighted in their work to show the mid century modern functionality that Barrágan incorporated.

features

The home features vast planes and simple lines that have become famous through out Barrágan’s work. Some of the most notable elements of this particular house is the use of earth tones, and bright pink walls. The most famous and central point of the building is the sprawling courtyard that showcases and expansive pool and signature water features.
